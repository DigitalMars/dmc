STLSoft for Digital Mars - Help

Documentation

The documentation of the STLSoft libraries for the Digital Mars compiler is available in two forms:

  • Compiled HTML Help

    • A Win32 Compiled HTML Help module (.CHM) - stlsoft.chm - is located in the help subdirectory of the STLSoft part of your Digital Mars compiler distribution. This file is updated with each version of the Digital Mars C/C++ compiler.

  • Online HTML Help

    • For up-to-the-minute help, or to access help from a non-Windows system, you can access online help in the form of HTML (web-pages). This comes in two forms

    • A full frame-based tree-view at http://stlsoft.digitalmars.com/help/index.html. Note that this has a very large script to handle the tree navigation, and Internet Explorer will warn you about this and ask you whether it should disable the script (you should not).
    • If you're running a system low-on memory, or you don't want scripts running, or you just don't like frame-based views (and who does?!) you can access a straightforward non-frame version at http://stlsoft.digitalmars.com/help/main.html

    Newsgroup

    Digital Mars have kindly provided the STLSoft newsgroup, which you're invited to visit and contribute questions, observations, criticisms and feature requests.

    Frequently Asked Questions

    If you cannot find the information you require in the documentation, or on the newsgroup, you can look at the FAQ. Greg Peet has kindly volunteered his time, effort and server space to look after the STLSoft FAQ, which is located at stlsoft.gregpeet.com.

    Email

    If you can't find what you want in the documentation, on the newsgroup, or in the FAQ, you are most welcome to email your concerns to admin@stlsoft.org and we'll do our best to reply to you in a timely fashion. (We'll even try and give you a useful response, but that's not always guaranteed!)
